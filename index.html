10.4 — Conic Sections

Parabolas, Ellipses, and Hyperbolas in standard and translated forms

Parabola (H)\(y^2=4px\): vertex \((0,0)\), focus \((p,0)\), directrix \(x=-p\); opens right if \(p>0\)
Parabola (V)\(x^2=4py\): vertex \((0,0)\), focus \((0,p)\), directrix \(y=-p\); opens up if \(p>0\)
Shifted Para.\((y-k)^2=4p(x-h)\) or \((x-h)^2=4p(y-k)\); vertex at \((h,k)\)
Ellipse\(\dfrac{x^2}{a^2}+\dfrac{y^2}{b^2}=1\), \(a>b>0\); \(c^2=a^2-b^2\); foci \((\pm c,0)\); eccentricity \(e=c/a<1\)
Hyperbola (H)\(\dfrac{x^2}{a^2}-\dfrac{y^2}{b^2}=1\); \(c^2=a^2+b^2\); asymptotes \(y=\pm\dfrac{b}{a}x\)
Hyperbola (V)\(\dfrac{y^2}{a^2}-\dfrac{x^2}{b^2}=1\); asymptotes \(y=\pm\dfrac{a}{b}x\)

2
Since \(p=-2<0\), the parabola opens down.
3
Vertex: \((0,0)\); Focus: \((0,p)=(0,-2)\); Directrix: \(y=-p=2\).
Answer: Vertex \((0,0)\), Focus \((0,-2)\), Directrix \(y=2\), opens down.

11.1 — Rectangular Coordinates in 3-Space; Spheres

Distance formula, midpoints, and equations of spheres in \(\mathbb{R}^3\)

Distance\(d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2+(z_2-z_1)^2}\)
Midpoint\(M=\left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2},\frac{y_1+y_2}{2},\frac{z_1+z_2}{2}\right)\)
Sphere\((x-h)^2+(y-k)^2+(z-\ell)^2=r^2\); center \((h,k,\ell)\), radius \(r\)
Complete Square\(x^2+bx = \left(x+\frac{b}{2}\right)^2-\frac{b^2}{4}\)

Problems

9 Find the distance between the points \(A(2,3,-1)\) and \(B(4,1,3)\), and find the midpoint of segment \(AB\).
Formula: \(d=\sqrt{\Delta x^2+\Delta y^2+\Delta z^2}\)
1
\(d=\sqrt{(4-2)^2+(1-3)^2+(3-(-1))^2}=\sqrt{4+4+16}=\sqrt{24}=2\sqrt{6}\).
2
Midpoint: \(M=\left(\frac{2+4}{2},\frac{3+1}{2},\frac{-1+3}{2}\right)=(3,2,1)\).
Answer: Distance \(=2\sqrt{6}\approx 4.90\); Midpoint \(M=(3,2,1)\).

Method: Complete the square in each variable

2
Complete: \((x-1)^2-1+(y+2)^2-4+(z-1)^2-1=3\).
3
\((x-1)^2+(y+2)^2+(z-1)^2=9\).
Answer: Center \((1,-2,1)\), Radius \(r=3\).

14 Find the center and radius: \(2x^2+2y^2+2z^2-4x+8y-16z-1=0\).

11.2 — Vectors

Component form, magnitude, unit vectors, and vector arithmetic

Component Form\(\mathbf{v}=\langle v_1,v_2,v_3\rangle\); from \(A(x_1,y_1,z_1)\) to \(B(x_2,y_2,z_2)\): \(\mathbf{v}=\langle x_2-x_1,y_2-y_1,z_2-z_1\rangle\)
Magnitude\(\|\mathbf{v}\|=\sqrt{v_1^2+v_2^2+v_3^2}\)
Unit Vector\(\hat{\mathbf{v}}=\dfrac{\mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{v}\|}\)
Arithmetic\(\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v}=\langle u_1+v_1,u_2+v_2,u_3+v_3\rangle\); \(k\mathbf{v}=\langle kv_1,kv_2,kv_3\rangle\)
From Angle\(\mathbf{v}=\|\mathbf{v}\|\langle\cos\theta,\sin\theta\rangle\) for 2D vectors at angle \(\theta\)
Standard Basis\(\mathbf{i}=\langle1,0,0\rangle,\;\mathbf{j}=\langle0,1,0\rangle,\;\mathbf{k}=\langle0,0,1\rangle\)

Problems

DomainSet of all \((x,y)\) for which \(f(x,y)\) is defined (no division by zero, no negative sqrt, etc.)
Level Curves\(f(x,y)=k\) gives a curve in the \(xy\)-plane for each constant \(k\)
Level Surfaces\(f(x,y,z)=k\) gives a surface in \(\mathbb{R}^3\)
RangeSet of all output values \(f(x,y)\) as \((x,y)\) varies over the domain

Problems

1Find the domain and range of \(f(x,y)=\sqrt{x+y}\).
1
Domain: need \(x+y\geq0\), i.e. the half-plane \(\{(x,y):y\geq-x\}\).
2
Range: \(\sqrt{x+y}\geq0\), and can be any non-negative value. Range \(=[0,\infty)\).
Answer: Domain: \(x+y\geq0\); Range: \([0,\infty)\).
3Find the domain and range of \(f(x,y)=\dfrac{1}{x^2+y^2}\).
1
Domain: \(x^2+y^2\neq0\), i.e., all \((x,y)\neq(0,0)\). Domain = \(\mathbb{R}^2\setminus\{(0,0)\}\).
2
Range: \(x^2+y^2>0\) so \(f>0\); it can be arbitrarily large or small. Range \(=(0,\infty)\).
Answer: Domain: \(\mathbb{R}^2\setminus\{(0,0)\}\); Range: \((0,\infty)\).
6Find the domain of \(f(x,y)=\ln(y-x^2)\).
1
Need \(y-x^2>0\Rightarrow y>x^2\). Domain is the open region above the parabola \(y=x^2\).
Answer: \(\{(x,y): y>x^2\}\).
7Describe the level curves of \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2\) for \(k=0,1,4,9\).
1
Level curve \(f=k\): \(x^2+y^2=k\). For \(k=0\): point \((0,0)\). For \(k=1,4,9\): circles of radius \(1, 2, 3\).
Answer: Concentric circles centered at origin, radii \(0,1,2,3\).
8Describe the level curves of \(f(x,y)=x-y\) for \(k=-2,-1,0,1,2\).
1
\(x-y=k\Rightarrow y=x-k\). These are lines with slope 1, \(y\)-intercepts \(-k=2,1,0,-1,-2\).
Answer: Family of parallel lines \(y=x-k\) with slope 1.
18Find the domain and range of \(f(x,y,z)=\sqrt{9-x^2-y^2-z^2}\).
1
Domain: \(9-x^2-y^2-z^2\geq0\Rightarrow x^2+y^2+z^2\leq9\). Solid ball of radius 3.
2
Range: \(f\in[0,3]\).
Answer: Domain: closed ball \(x^2+y^2+z^2\leq9\); Range: \([0,3]\).
67Sketch level curves of \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2\) and identify the surface \(z=x^2+y^2\).
1
Level curves \(z=k\): circles \(x^2+y^2=k\) of radius \(\sqrt{k}\) for \(k\geq0\).
2
The surface is a circular paraboloid opening upward.
Answer: Level curves are concentric circles; surface is a circular paraboloid.
